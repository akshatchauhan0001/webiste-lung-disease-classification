PNEUMONIA
"Pneumonia strikes the lungs with silent force, demanding prompt care and vigilance to restore breath and vitality swiftly."
PNEUMONIA
Pneumonia, an inflammatory lung condition, has been known since ancient times and was first clinically described by Hippocrates around 460 BC. It causes symptoms like fever, chills, chest pain, cough with mucus, and difficulty breathing. Common causes include bacterial, viral, or fungal infections, with Streptococcus pneumoniae being a primary bacterial culprit. Treatment varies by cause, including antibiotics, antivirals, or antifungals, along with supportive care like oxygen therapy. Advances in medical science, including vaccines, have significantly reduced the mortality rate of pneumonia, making early detection and treatment vital for recovery.

Detection Time
